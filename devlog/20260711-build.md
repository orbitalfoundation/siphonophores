## The parameter space (the crux)

A colony = an ordered chain along a stem: `float → nectosome (bells) → siphosome
(cormidia)`. Suborders differ only in which regions exist. Bells go round↔prismatic
via a superellipse exponent; the stem bows / coils (Apolemia) / sags; the siphosome
repeats a cormidium (gastrozooid + tentacle±lure, palpon skirt, bracts). Real species
override only their distinctive leaves. This decomposition is why one rig can be a
man o' war *and* a giant coiling Apolemia *and* a glassy Hippopodius.

## What went right / wrong

- **Research fanned out well.** One research agent spawned four clusters covering all
  suborders + a dedicated Nanomia-locomotion dive — sourced morphology, colour, and
  the pulse numbers (~3.9 Hz, jet:refill 1:1, wave apex→base, ~9 bells) straight into
  params. Corrections it caught: Apolemia's coil is a body posture not spiral bell-
  packing (Forskalia is the true spiral); Resomia lures are green not red.
- **Bug: the float ate the stem.** The float's size fraction was doubling as reserved
  stem arclength, leaving a dead gap before the feeding zooids. Fix: the float sits
  *above* the apex and consumes no arclength.
- **Polish that mattered most:** global luminosity lift on the gel, and swapping flat
  `MeshBasicMaterial` gastrozooids for self-lit `MeshStandardMaterial` — turned flat
  orange dots into glowing internal organs. Physophora's skirt went from a dense blob
  to two clean palpon whorls by dropping cormidia 4→2 and enlarging the palpons.

## Workflow on this box (important — this was the point)

The full autonomous loop works headlessly on Ubuntu, no laptop needed:
- **Render + SEE output:** Playwright's bundled Chromium
  (`~/.cache/ms-playwright/chromium-1228/chrome-linux64/chrome`) driven over CDP
  (`scripts/render.mjs`), `--use-angle=vulkan` on the real GPU (`/dev/dri`). Screenshots
  to PNG that I can actually read back and judge. This is the unlock.
- **Deploy without a token:** the exe.dev **gateway SSH** is authorized by this box's
  key (`ssh exe.dev new/share …`), so no `deploy/.api-token` was needed — provision +
  deploy + make-public + autodeploy all ran from here.
- 32 cores / 30 GB / passwordless sudo / real GPU — long sessions are comfortable.
- Reusable across future parametric projects: copy `scripts/render.mjs` + the exe.dev
  `deploy/` scripts, retarget the VM/repo names.
